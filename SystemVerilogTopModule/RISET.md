Realizing the Omega Chip design requires fabrication methodologies far more precise than current light-based lithography. The Programmable Reality workflow leverages DNA origami as scaffolding to position electronic components at the nanometer scale.

In the Omega Chip fabrication process, DNA staple strands are used to hold structures in place. When a circuit path on the Omega Chip breaks due to mechanical stress or thermal degradation, built-in resistance sensors detect the increased resistance.

The self-healing control system then activates microfluidic pumps to flow EGaIn into the damaged area. The liquid metal automatically fills the broken gap due to its unique surface tension and ability to wet untreated polymer surfaces. The use of liquid metal also aids in chip thermal management. The Photonic NoC requires extreme temperature stability (resolution down to 0.001°C) to maintain wavelength lock on ring resonators.
